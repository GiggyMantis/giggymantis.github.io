Yukatati's alphabet has nineteen letters— fourteen consonants and five vowels.

  • a - "a" as in US cloth or UK bath
  • c - "ce" as in child
  • e - "e" as in face
  • f - "fe" as in foot
  • g - "eg" as in sing
  • h - "he" as in hold
  • i - "i" as in fleece
  • k - "ke" as in scab
  • l - "le" as in line
  • m - "me" as in mage
  • n - "ne" as in neat
  • o - "o" as in goat
  • p - "pe" as in space
  • s - "se" as in scissors
  • t - "te" as in stare
  • u - "u" as in goose
  • y - "ye" as in yes
  • w - "we" as in wet

Phonology

Yukatati has a very small phonology, combined with simple phonotactics that make it compatible with the majority of the world's languages.

Phonotactics

Yukatati has a CV(N) syllable structure. Words are allowed to start with vowels, but not with just a nasal.

V is allowed to be any monopthong or one of the four diphthongs.

C is allowed to be any of the single consonants listed above, or the cluster /kw/. This is the only allowed cluster.

The sound /ŋ/ is not allowed to begin a word. This rule is to ensure compatibility with the large amount of languages that ban /ŋ/ word-initially, including the two most spoken languages, English and Mandarin.
