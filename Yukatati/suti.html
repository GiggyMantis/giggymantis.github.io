Umug Suti

Yukatati has a functional alignment, with very little inflection. It does however feature a large number of derivitive affixes to form new words. It has modifier-modified (i.e. adjective-noun) order, but is otherwise head-initial (SVO word order, prepositions, etc.) Pronouns are not declined for case, but are for person and number. Pronoun dropping is proscribed. There are no indefinite or definite articles, but many other types of determiners exist with similar use cases. Yukatati has a three-way demonstrative distinction, specifically proximal, medial, and distal.

Onomatopoeia

Onomatopoeia are treated like normal content words, except they are always reduplicated.

Genitive Constructions

Possessive phrases with pronouns are constructed by using the possessive pronoun as an adjective. Pronominal genitive phrases, nominal possessive phrases, and nominal genitive phrases are made by using the preposition :ka.

Plurality

Plural words are formed by reduplicating the [first three syllables of the] noun. Adjectives and verbs do not agree in number with nouns.
