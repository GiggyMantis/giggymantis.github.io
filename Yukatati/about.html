About Yukatati

Yukatati is a :constructed :IAL which I started in 2024 with the core principles of anti-colonialism, anti-imperialism, and collaboration. It has the primary gimmick of the fact that source languages are picked for each word with a likelihood relative to their current speakership. Especially in its phonemic inventory, that is, its sounds, Yukatati is inspired by principles of minimalism and especially the language of :toki pona.

After a break over 2025, in January of 2026 we reignited the project and began to develop with the actual goal of growing a community speaking the language.

Wait, but aren't you against IALs?

Well, yes and no. I am staunchly against ideologies such as :finvenkism. I do believe that any kind of international adoption of an auxlang where there is a social pressure to learn the language will lead to language death, but I do think the concept of having languages that can be voluntarily learned, without social pressure or hierarchy, to communicate internationally with others is a good idea and a noble project.

Overall, right now I am something of a :Raumist in regards to Yukatati. I want to grow the community.

:What about zonal interlangs?

I'm actually a pretty big supporter of zonal interlangs like :Interslavic for one reason and one reason only:

The goal isn't to make people learn it. The goal is to make a language that is understandable to speakers of one or more language, so the only reason someone has to learn Interslavic is to produce content that can be understood naturally by native speakers of those languages.

Wikipedia calls these "Pan-[family] languages" which is an interesting choice. It also universally refers to interlangs as "auxlangs." Again, odd choice.

Anyways, I think these are very cool, and I've attempted to make both Romance and Germanic interlangs in the past.
