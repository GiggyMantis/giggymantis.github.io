If There Is A Heaven

February 9th, 2026

If there is a Heaven,
It’s not for You or Me.
The vaulted firmament’s columns
Bow out in disrepair
For the fullness they expect
Much more did they receive.

No, if there is a Heaven,
It’s for Beast, and Beast alone.
Up there lives every slaughtered calf,
Every worm that’s cut in half,
And in their rivers fishes breathe
Water clearer than in life they did receive
For chicks culled young are grown-up there,
Great roosters’ cries fill the morning air
As pigeons and rats swarm the cities,
And even leeches sit there pretty,
For every fly swatted down by man,
A thousand more were killed again,
And every ant burnt by a child,
Can now once more run amok and wild

The dodos and mammoths find it strange,
How none more of their kind they see again,
They roam, the auks and Huia birds,
The quaggas, aurochsen, and thylacines,

No, Heaven is not made for you or me,
But for the humble bumblebee,
And every lamb that’s put to slaughter,
And every ape and lemur’s daughters,
The coelacanths and marlins swim,
In rivers; wine- and honey-brimmed

And every human who’s tried’s been nixxed
And cast into the River Styx,

But God has yet to find vermin
Whose very soul isn’t free from sin.

Lightning

July 14th, 2025

That putrid stench of blood and air,
Fills up my nose, I do swear,
And rightfully takes the daughter in arms,
And protects her from all our pagan charms.

A gaucho speaks in gristled sound,
“Mijita no necesita aun
pensarlo, mucho menos oírlo”
The dead of night and of day both

Run for god, and when it ends
The electors of god will make amends.

Sunlight

May 24th, 2025

Can you pour sunlight in a cup?
Filling up drop, by drop, by drop?
What about the ethereal summer breeze?
The one that sings as she sways the trees.

Can I catch the dreams you dream?
Can we freeze and store hot steam?
Put the Philharmonic in a box?
And keep it safe, with keyless locks.

War Memorial

March 26th, 2025

A war memorial stands
Tall and proud
Remembering our veterans

No rememberance plans
For the screaming crowd
Burnt dead in their settlements

In cancerous lands
Stands a lone, old cow
Remembering the denizens

Memento occidere— “remember that you must kill”

Ocean

March 24th, 2025

The world is an ocean and I have a raft
But on it I can only watch people drowning on the path
That the Gods of the Sea laid out
Underwater, solely to torture, no doubt

And occasionally
The waves get me

And the blind shrimp snap
Like Venus fly traps
At my feet and knees until I’m
Able to resurface one more time

I have friends in the ocean and they like me
But I can’t live my whole life on the sea
