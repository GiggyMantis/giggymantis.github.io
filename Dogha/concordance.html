                                                                                                                                                                        • ževa

                                                                                                                                                                          noun - large insect, large bug (includes spiders, scorpions, lobsters, etc.)

                                                                                                                                                                          the word ževa is of partially disputed meaning; in the context of the Voslhemow verse where it is most prominent, the next verse talks about them weaving (which implies it means spiders), but the previous verses this one is contrasted to explicitly talk about small bugs like ants rather than large bugs like beetles. There was also very prominent sericulture in this time period/cultural context, so silkworms' ability to weave would have also been relevant. Non-Voslhemic texts tend to use it as a catch-all phrase equivalent to english "bug," but also including seaborne arthropods. One prominent suggestion is that "ṇyṣelaxej hyzṭa" was used to mean primarily herbivorous, omnivorous, or parasitic bugs, like ants, aphids, butterflies, ticks, and lice, while "hyzževa" was used to mean primarily carnivorous bugs like spiders, scorpions, ladybugs, etc. This comes from V 2:6, which explicitly describes the "hyzṭy" as eating and thus holding dominion over plants.
